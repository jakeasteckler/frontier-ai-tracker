Methodology & Definitions

"Frontier Lab Military Partnerships" = a frontier AI lab's technology is explicitly deployed to, contracted by, formally made available for, or plausibly positioned for use by a nation's military, defense ministry, intelligence services, or defense contractors for national-security purposes. Details note whether evidence is a contract, MOU, infrastructure project, or reported outreach.

"Foreign Military AI Partners" = a documented contract, MOU, partnership, deployment, demonstration, or reported defense-relevant engagement between an external AI/cloud/defense technology company, defense supplier, or state-linked technology provider and a country's military, defense ministry, intelligence services, or defense industry for AI-specific or autonomy-adjacent capabilities — autonomous systems, AI-powered targeting, AI-enabled electronic warfare, AI surveillance, AI/cloud infrastructure, etc. General defense contracts without an explicit AI/autonomy component are excluded.

"Domestic Military AI" = the country, military, or locally based force has a publicly documented indigenous military AI program, research center, domestically developed autonomous weapons system, or defense-relevant drone/autonomy activity where no external AI partner is identified. Drone-only cases are hedged in the details and should not be read as confirmed AI procurement unless the source says so.

"AI Strategy / Policy" = an officially published government or military document, directive, formal statement, or institution that outlines or signals defense-relevant AI activity. Includes dedicated defense AI strategies, broader defense modernization plans mentioning AI/autonomy, national AI strategies with defense/security implications, and formal defense AI research hubs.

Frontier AI labs tracked: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind (Gemini), Meta (Llama), Mistral, xAI, DeepSeek.

Inclusion criteria: Countries in or near the top 50 by military expenditure (latest SIPRI data) OR top 50 by active troop size, plus countries with documented defense-AI activity or policy relevance, excluding the US and China.

Data sources: Defense budget figures use SIPRI Military Expenditure Database current US$ values, primarily 2025 where available; recent-unavailable cases retain estimates or the latest suitable public figure. Active troop figures use IISS Military Balance active-duty personnel as the primary baseline, with Global Firepower used where IISS is unavailable or a current conflict-state estimate is more suitable. Deal information from official press releases, company announcements, defense journalism, and think tank research (2024–2026).
