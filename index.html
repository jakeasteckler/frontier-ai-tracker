Methodology & Definitions

"Frontier Lab Deal (Non-Military)" = a formally announced MOU, contract, investment partnership, or technology deployment between a frontier AI lab and a national government entity. Commercial availability alone does NOT count. These deals are government-level but not explicitly military in nature.

"Frontier Lab Deal (Military)" = a frontier AI lab's technology is explicitly deployed to, contracted by, or formally partnered with a nation's military, defense ministry, or intelligence services.

"Foreign Military AI Partner" = a confirmed contract or partnership between an external defense technology company and a country's military for AI-specific capabilities — autonomous systems, AI-powered targeting, AI-enabled electronic warfare, AI surveillance, etc. General defense contracts without an explicit AI component are excluded.

"Domestic Military AI" = the country has a publicly documented indigenous military AI program, research center, or domestically developed autonomous weapons system. These are tagged separately from external defense AI partnerships.

Inclusion criteria: Any country in the top 50 by military budget (SIPRI 2024) OR top 50 by active troop size, excluding the US and China.

Data sources: Military budget data from SIPRI 2024. Active troop data from IISS Military Balance & Global Firepower Index (2024–2025). Deal information from official press releases, company announcements, defense journalism, CSIS, and SIPRI (2024–2026).
